F1 Las Vegas
The Strip Is The Track

Nov 19–21, 2026 · Las Vegas Strip Circuit

Every November, the Strip closes and becomes one of F1's fastest circuits. Cars hit 212mph past casino doors. A three-day festival takes over the whole city. Here's how to do it right.

Pack for Cold — Not Vegas Heat
Race nights start at 8pm. By midnight, Strip temperatures hit 40–45°F (4–7°C). Everyone shows up expecting desert heat. November is different. Bring a real jacket, layers, and warm boots. You'll be standing outside for hours.

Plan Your Week

Race Week Schedule

Three days, six sessions, one race. The build is gradual — by Saturday night the whole Strip is electric.

Thursday Nov 19
Practice 1 — Evening session. Opening laps on the Strip circuit. Good night to find your bearings.
Practice 2 — Late night. Cars running under full city lights. Most atmospheric practice session.
Fan Zones Open — Team pop-ups activate. First merch drops. Strip starts transforming.
Friday Nov 20
Practice 3 — Teams fine-tune setups. Faster now.
Qualifying — Late night. This sets the grid. High intensity. If you're choosing one session to watch live, this is it.
Team Activations — Full team fan zone day. Simulator events, merch drops across casino floors.
Saturday Nov 21 · Race Day
Pre-Race Ceremonies — Opening show, driver parade, grid walk for credential holders.
Race · 8:00 PM — 50 laps on the Strip. Post-race: Steve Aoki at Hakkasan.

Know Before You Buy

Grandstand Guide

Three zones, very different experiences. The right seat depends on what you actually want to see.

Best Overall Seat
East Harmon Zone
Start/Finish Line · Pit Lane
Home of the PG1 Main Grandstand — the most complete F1 viewing experience on the circuit. Watch cars launch off the grid, pit lane drama unfold, and the race decided in real time.
  • PG1 Main Grandstand — start/finish line, pit lane views, F1 simulators nearby. The gold standard seat.
  • Row warning: Rows 17–20 have a support aisle that blocks sightlines. Request rows 1–16 or 21+.
  • Hello Kitty Grandstands — Turns 3–4 hairpin. More affordable, tight-corner action.
🌐 Best Atmosphere
T-Mobile Zone at Sphere
Turns 5–9 · Sphere Complex
Eight grandstands (SG1–SG8) wrapped around the Sphere. The most visually stunning zone on the circuit — the Sphere itself is part of the backdrop. Concert stage visible from key seats.
  • SG4 & SG5 — best of the zone. Turns 7–8 action plus full Sphere concert stage view.
  • SG1–SG3: earlier turns, closer to entry.
  • Post-race: the Sphere concert stage lights up directly in your sightline.
Pit Lane Straight
West Harmon Zone
Turns 12–17 · Pit Straight
HG1 and the Lewis Hamilton Grandstand (HG3) face the pit lane straight. Good views of pit entry/exit drama. Slightly more sheltered from wind than the East Harmon zone.
  • HG3 Lewis Hamilton Grandstand — named after the most decorated driver in the sport.
  • Pit lane entry visible — great for strategy-watching.
  • Often better prices than East Harmon for similar pit action.

General Admission Zones

GA tickets give you track access without an assigned seat. Arrive early, move between sessions, find your spot.

T-Mobile GA at Sphere
Mobile access inside the Sphere zone. Multiple vantage points across the T-Mobile Zone. Best atmosphere of the GA options.
Flamingo GA Zone
Turns 3–4 straight, mid-Strip location. Good value. Easy walk from multiple hotels. Tight corner action.
Heineken GA+
Turn 4 / Koval Straight. Bleacher seating included — not pure standing. Beer garden atmosphere. The most relaxed GA option.

The Smart Strategy

Hotel Track Views

The circuit runs through the hotel district. Certain rooms look directly onto the track — and the right choice beats a $3,000 grandstand ticket.

The Math Nobody Tells You
Balcony > Grandstand
A Cosmopolitan terrace room runs around $800–$1,200/night during race week. A PG1 grandstand seat runs $3,000–$8,000 for the full weekend. For the balcony, you're in the room anyway. The track view is free. Cars pass at 200mph directly in front of you. You have drinks in hand.

Gold Standard Track View
Cosmopolitan — Boulevard Tower
The most-requested F1 track view hotel in Las Vegas. Oversized terraces on the Strip straightaway — cars pass at 200mph+ directly outside your room. Book early; these rooms sell out months before other Strip hotels during race week.
Turns 12–16 visible Strip straightaway 200mph+ cars
Pro tip: Request rooms ending in -88 for the best viewing angle. Boulevard Tower only — the other Cosmo towers don't have the same sightlines.
Versailles Balcony Rooms
Paris Las Vegas
High-floor Strip-facing rooms with clear sightlines to the action. The Eiffel Tower backdrop makes this the most photogenic track view in Vegas.
Turns 13–14 210mph+
Book: Versailles Balcony category. Request a high floor on the Strip side.
Panoramic View King · 30th floor+
Wynn Las Vegas
Elevated position looking down at the primary overtaking zone. The height advantage gives you a racing line perspective you don't get at track level.
Turns 1–2 Overtaking zone
Book: 30th floor minimum. Below that, other buildings can obstruct sightlines.
South Tower · Sphere views
Venetian / Palazzo
South Tower rooms face the Sphere turn complex. Further from the action than Cosmo or Paris, but elevated and with a Sphere backdrop that looks incredible at night.
Sphere Turn Elevated view

Tower 3 Balcony · Budget Pick
Signature at MGM
The best value track view option on the list. Tower 3 balconies face Turns 16–17. Significantly cheaper than Cosmo or Paris during race week. Good if the top picks are sold out.
Turns 16–17 Best value

Most Accessible · Guaranteed Views
Flamingo
The most accessible price point on this list. Request Strip-facing rooms and you're guaranteed to see the action. Not a premium view, but you're in the mix and the price difference is significant.
Strip facing GA Zone nearby

Free All Week

Fan Zones & Activations

F1 race week transforms the entire Strip. Team pop-ups, show cars, fan zones, and merch drops happen across multiple casinos — most of it free.

🏎️
Ferrari & Mercedes Experiences
Pop-up fan zones at Bellagio and ARIA. Simulator activations, car displays, merchandise. Free to enter, lines form fast in the mornings.
🎮
Williams Racing Fan Zone
New York-New York casino. One of the more accessible team activations — F1 simulators, team kit displays, signing appearances from support staff.
🔧
Haas F1 Show Car Appearances
The Haas show car does Strip walkabouts throughout the week — literally rolling through casino entrances and public areas. Location announced same-day on team socials.
🏁
F1 Simulators
Multiple simulator stations across the Strip at team activations and the official F1 fan zone. The PG1 grandstand area has simulators near the start/finish line.
👕
Team Merch Drops
Limited team merchandise drops happen across casino properties throughout the week. Times and locations announced on team social accounts the morning of. They go fast.
👗
Fashion Collabs
F1 Las Vegas draws luxury brand collabs. LVMH-connected brands and F1 teams have done exclusive Vegas drops in prior years. Check team announcements in the week before race week.
💡

Fan zone strategy: Use practice sessions (Thu–Fri) for fan zone exploration — it's less crowded and you can move between activations freely. Save Qualifying and Race day energy for the circuit itself.

After the Checkered Flag

Race Week Nightlife

This is Las Vegas. The parties are baked into the event. Here are the three worth knowing.

Hakkasan
MGM Grand · The Main Event
The race week nightclub. All three nights are booked with big names. Get tickets in advance — Saturday post-race fills up within hours.
  • Thursday — Loud Luxury
  • Friday — DJ Pauly D
  • Saturday (post-race) — Steve Aoki
Pinky Ring
Bellagio · Live Music Nightly
Bruno Mars's venue at Bellagio. Live bands from 9pm nightly throughout race week. More dinner-party than nightclub — easier to get into, higher quality experience, better conversation.
  • Live bands nightly from 9pm
  • All race week — Thu through Sat
  • Reservations recommended but walk-ins possible
Smooth Operator
ALIBI at ARIA · Carlos Sainz Party
Named after Carlos Sainz's walk-out song. Runs all week at ALIBI Lounge — relaxed enough to actually get into, with a genuine F1 energy that the mainstream clubs can't match.
  • Wed–Sun, 2pm–2am
  • ALIBI Lounge, ARIA hotel
  • Most accessible race-themed event of the week
