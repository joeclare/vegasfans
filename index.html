UFC Fight Week in Vegas:
Don't Wing It

Vegas is the home of the UFC. You're not just going to a fight — you're walking into the sport's spiritual headquarters for a week. Here's how to do it right.

Know Before You Go

The Two Venues

There are two places UFC fights happen in Vegas. Only one is built for visiting fans.

Main Stage — PPV Events

T-Mobile Arena

3780 Las Vegas Blvd S · Between Park MGM and New York-New York

20,000 seats, right on the Strip. Every major numbered PPV — the title fights, the main events — happens here. If you're flying to Vegas for a UFC event, you're almost certainly going here.

🎯

Seat tip: The cage is raised. Floor seats mean looking up at the action. Best view: sections 5/6 or 15/16, roughly 5 rows up from floor level — eye level with the octagon.

Private Studio — Fight Night Cards

UFC APEX

6650 El Camino Rd · Off-Strip near the airport

The UFC's own production facility. ~1,800 capacity, but almost none of those tickets are for you. Fight Night events run here — tickets mostly go to fighters' families, media, and industry.

⚠️

Bottom line: If you somehow land a ticket, go — it's intimate and special. But don't build a Vegas trip around trying to get in.

Stack Your Days

The Fight Week Timeline

UFC PPV week has a rhythm. Once you know it, you can stack your days instead of just showing up Saturday and going home Sunday.

Tue
Wed

Fighters Arrive — Open Workouts

UFC often holds open workouts at a casino property — historically the MGM Grand casino floor. Free to attend, informal, you can get close. Worth going if you're already in town. Follow UFC on social for same-day announcements.

Thu

Official UFC Open Workouts

The organized version — fighters run combinations on pads in front of fans. Free. Public. Usually held at a casino or arena event space. Check UFC.com closer to the event for the exact location.

Fri

Official Weigh-Ins ⭐ Don't Skip This

Free. Public. High energy. Fighters stare each other down, crowds react to every body reveal, and there's always at least one moment that ends up on SportsCenter. The most underrated free event of fight week. Show up early — lines form fast.

Sat
AM

Ceremonial Weigh-Ins & Face-Offs

A second, more theatrical version — fighters are re-hydrated, walkout music plays, crowds are louder. Free and public. Good primer for the night's energy.

Sat
PM

Early Prelims — ~1–2 PM PT

If you're watching from a sportsbook or bar, stake out your spot early. Westgate and Circa fill up well before the main card.

Sat
Night

Main Card — ~7–10 PM PT

Give yourself time to get to the arena. Traffic and pedestrian congestion around the Strip is real on PPV night. Plan to arrive by 5:30–6 PM for anything close to the arena.

Where to Be

Bars, Books & Watch Parties

Three categories, different experiences. Pick based on what kind of fight night you want.

Near T-Mobile Arena

2-Min Walk

Beerhaus at The Park

The Park outdoor mall · Next to T-Mobile

German beer hall, long communal tables, wall of TVs. The best pre-game option near the arena. Gets loud, gets packed early on PPV nights — exactly what you want to build fight energy. Don't expect to walk in at 6 PM and find a seat.

10-Min Walk

Flanker Kitchen + Sports Bar

Mandalay Bay Resort

50+ TVs and a 70-foot video wall. UFC puts on official viewing parties here for select events. Good spot to watch early prelims on Paramount+ before heading to the arena for the main card. Check ahead for reservations on fight night.

Right Outside

Toshiba Plaza

Outside T-Mobile Arena main entrance

The outdoor plaza between the arena and Park MGM. Pre-fight and post-fight, this is the gathering point. Not a bar — just the place to be when the crowd builds. By 5 PM on big PPV nights, it's a scene.

Best Sportsbooks for Watching

Gold Standard

Westgate SuperBook

Off-Strip (east) · Uber required

30,000 sq ft. 220-foot 4K video wall. 350+ seats. The most famous sportsbook in the world for a reason — the UFC looks incredible here and the betting energy during a title fight is unlike anywhere else. Worth the Uber.

Center Strip

Caesars Palace Sportsbook

Caesars Palace · Las Vegas Blvd

142-foot HD LED screen with directional sound. Right in the center of the Strip — if you want to watch fights without leaving the main action, this is the move. Comfortable, well-run, great sight lines.

Sleeper Pick

Wynn Race & Sports Book

Wynn Las Vegas · North Strip

Smaller and more upscale than Westgate, but the best food of any sportsbook on the Strip. If you want to watch the fight without fighting for a seat in a cavernous room, the Wynn is the pick.

Watch Parties

Best in Vegas

Stadium Swim at Circa

Circa Resort & Casino · Downtown Las Vegas

143-foot screen. A pool. A sportsbook. All in one venue. Circa hosts UFC PPV watch parties and they sell out. The atmosphere is absurd in the best way. Book ahead — it fills up weeks before a big fight. Also the best alternative if you don't have arena tickets.

Free Entry

Buffalo Wild Wings

Strip · Next to Planet Hollywood

Always shows UFC, always has specials, free to walk in. Not the most glamorous option, but it's consistent, easy, and there's always a fight crowd here. Good overflow option if everywhere else is packed.

Game Day Logistics

Getting to T-Mobile Arena

🚗 Uber / Lyft

Use the Park MGM or New York-New York entrance side for drop-off and pickup. Don't try to get dropped on Las Vegas Blvd — traffic gridlock on fight night will cost you 20 extra minutes in the car.

🚶 Walking

MGM Grand: ~5 min

Aria / Vdara: ~7 min

Cosmopolitan: ~10 min

Bellagio / Caesars: ~15 min

🅿️ Parking

Aria's parking garage is the closest paid option — expect $15–20 on fight nights. MGM Grand also has parking if you want to walk through the casino. Don't attempt to park on Las Vegas Blvd.

💡

Crowd tip: Leave ~45 minutes before the main event ends to beat the rush — or stay until the venue fully empties (it clears faster than you'd think). The worst option is leaving right when everyone else does.

Where to Sleep

Where to Stay

Book early. Rooms that cost $150 on a random Thursday will run $400–600 on PPV fight weekend. Eight to twelve weeks out is the window to lock in reasonable rates.

Walking Distance
  • Park MGM — literally attached to the arena complex
  • New York-New York — across the plaza, steps away
  • MGM Grand — 5-min walk, tons of food options
5–10 Min Walk
  • Aria — one of the nicer Strip properties
  • Vdara — quieter, no casino floor, all-suite
  • Cosmopolitan — great location, higher price point
Best Value
  • Excalibur — short walk or Uber, significantly cheaper
  • Luxor — same price range, walkable to the arena

Good base if you're managing the budget without sacrificing location.

Off-Strip
  • Palms — genuinely underrated, good rooms
  • Rio — cheapest rates, Uber required everywhere

Uber required but savings are real. Fine if you're just sleeping there.

Getting In

Tickets

When to Buy

PPV fight tickets typically go on sale ~8 weeks before the event. Buy then if you're sure you're going — prices increase steadily as fight week approaches.

Resale Strategy

StubHub and Vivid Seats are the reliable options. There's often a price dip the week of the fight as sellers who bought to flip get nervous and drop their prices. If you're flexible on seats, waiting can work in your favor.

Best Seats Strategy

🎯

Best value view: Sections 5/6 or 15/16, ~5 rows up from floor. You're at cage height — the action is in front of you, not below you.

⚠️

Avoid floor seats unless you don't mind looking up. The cage is elevated. Floor looks like a bad angle when you're actually there.

💡

Upper bowl (200s): Still a great atmosphere and a real view of the octagon. Don't let anyone talk you out of the upper deck at a UFC event — the crowd energy is the same everywhere.
