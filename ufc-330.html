July heat warning: Vegas in July regularly hits 105°F+. Plan indoor activities during the day. Stay hydrated. The walk from your hotel to the arena at 7 PM will still be 95°F — dress accordingly and know where your water is.

All Week

The Fight Week Game Plan

UFC week in Vegas is its own event. Stack the whole week, not just Saturday night.

Tue
Wed

Open Workouts (Informal)

Watch for announcements — UFC fighters typically do informal open workouts at casino floors. MGM Grand has historically been the go-to. Free, often not heavily promoted, and genuinely worth attending. Follow UFC on social for same-day announcements.

Thu

Official Open Workouts

Free public event. Fighters from the main card run through their pads in front of fans. Gets a real crowd. Show up early to get a good spot. Location TBA — check UFC.com as the event approaches.

Fri

Official Weigh-Ins ⭐ Best Free Event of the Week

Free, public, high energy. Fighters are at their most intense — the crowd reacts to every scale reading. Plan to get there 45 minutes early. Lines form fast for PPV events. Location TBA, check UFC.com.

Sat
AM

Ceremonial Weigh-Ins & Face-Offs

Fighters re-hydrated, walkout music, louder crowds. Free, public. Good primer for the night's energy.

Sat
AFT

Get in Position — Early Prelims ~3:30 PM PT

T-Mobile area gets packed by early afternoon. Toshiba Plaza becomes a scene by 5 PM on big PPV nights. Don't plan to roll up casually at 6 PM.

Sat
Night

Main Card — ~7:00 PM PT

Fight night. If you have tickets, be inside by 6:30. If watching elsewhere, Stadium Swim and Westgate will be packed — show up well before main card starts.

Fight Day

Where to Pre-Game

Everything here is within a 10-minute window of T-Mobile Arena.

2-Min Walk · First Choice

Beerhaus at The Park

The Park outdoor mall · Adjacent to T-Mobile

German beer hall, communal tables, loud — exactly what you want to build fight energy. Gets full early on PPV nights. Get there by 4:30 PM or you're standing.

Right Outside

Toshiba Plaza

Outside T-Mobile Arena main entrance

No reservations, no cover. The natural gathering point for fight crowds. By 5 PM on PPV nights this plaza is electric. Note: outdoor in July = very hot.

10-Min Walk

Flanker Kitchen + Sports Bar

Mandalay Bay Resort

70-foot video wall, full bar. Good spot to post up for early prelims before heading to the arena for the main card. Check ahead for reservations on fight night.

Overflow Option

New York-New York Casino Bars

NYNY · Right next to the arena

Multiple bars on the casino floor, easy access, no cover. Good overflow option if Beerhaus is packed, or a quick stop before walking to the arena.

🌡️

July note: Pre-gaming outdoors at Toshiba Plaza in July means being in 100°F+ heat. Beerhaus has AC and shade. Plan accordingly.

No Ticket? No Problem

Best Alternatives for UFC 330

UFC 330 is still worth experiencing in Vegas from outside the arena. These are the three best options.

Best in Vegas

Stadium Swim at Circa

Circa Resort & Casino · Downtown Las Vegas

143-foot screen pool party setup. The best non-arena UFC experience in Las Vegas. Book a spot well in advance for numbered PPV events — they sell out.

For the Hardcore Fan

Westgate SuperBook

Westgate Las Vegas · Off-Strip (east)

220-foot 4K video wall, 30,000 sq ft. If you want to be surrounded by people who have money on the fight and actually know what they're watching, this is your spot.

Center Strip

Caesars Palace Sportsbook

Caesars Palace · Las Vegas Blvd

142-foot screen, great atmosphere, right in the center of the Strip. Easy access, no planning required beyond showing up.
