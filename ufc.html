The Venues

Know Where You're Going

Two venues. Very different experiences. Most visiting fans are going to exactly one of them.

PPV Events

T-Mobile Arena

20,000 seats. Right on the Strip between Park MGM and New York-New York. Every major numbered PPV — the title fights, the main events you've been watching buildup videos for — happens here.

📌

The cage is raised. Floor seats mean looking up at the action. Sweet spot: sections 5/6 or 15/16, roughly 5 rows up from floor level — you're at cage height, looking straight across.

3780 Las Vegas Blvd S · Between Park MGM and New York-New York

Fight Night Cards

UFC APEX

UFC's own production facility, off-Strip near the airport. Holds about 1,800 people — but almost none of those tickets are for visiting fans. This is where Fight Night cards run, mostly for fighters' families, media, and industry people.

⚠️

Don't build a Vegas trip around trying to get APEX tickets. If you're flying in for UFC, you're almost certainly going to T-Mobile Arena.

6650 El Camino Rd · Off-Strip, near airport

Where to Watch & Hang

Near T-Mobile & Around Vegas

Near T-Mobile Arena

Best Pre-Game
Beerhaus at The Park
📍 Right next to the arena · The Park outdoor mall

German beer hall setup, long communal tables, solid tap list, wall of TVs. The best pure pre-game option near T-Mobile. Gets loud, gets packed — that's the point.

Sports Bar
Flanker Kitchen + Sports Bar
📍 Mandalay Bay · 10-min walk down Strip

Big screen setup, solid fight crowd. UFC has hosted official events here in the past — check their site for what's happening fight week.

The Gathering Point
Toshiba Plaza
📍 Between arena and Park MGM

The outdoor plaza between the arena and Park MGM. Pre-fight and post-fight, this is where the crowd gathers. Not a bar, but the place to be when the energy spills out.

Sportsbooks — Best for Watching on Screen

Off-Strip
Westgate SuperBook
📍 Off-Strip, eastern Vegas · Worth the Uber

One of the most famous sportsbooks in the world. Massive screen, serious betting energy on fight night. Worth the Uber if you want the full sportsbook experience.

Strip
Caesars Palace Race & Sports Book
📍 Center of the Strip

Big screen, center of the Strip. Best option if you don't want to leave the main action. Comfortable and well-run.

Strip
Wynn Race & Sports Book
📍 Wynn Las Vegas · Strip

More upscale, less crowded than the big rooms. Best food and drink of any sportsbook on the Strip.

Watch Parties

Best No-Ticket Option
Stadium Swim at Circa
📍 Downtown Las Vegas

143-foot screen, pool, sportsbook all in one. The atmosphere on PPV night is unlike anything else. Check Circa's site for whether they're hosting a watch party — they sell out fast when they do.

Always Open
Buffalo Wild Wings
📍 Planet Hollywood · On the Strip

Always shows UFC, always has specials, free to walk in. Not the most glamorous option but it's consistent, easy, and there's always a fight crowd here.

Logistics

Getting to T-Mobile Arena

🚗 Uber / Lyft
Use Park MGM or New York-New York Side

Don't try to get dropped on Las Vegas Blvd — traffic gridlock on fight night will cost you 20 extra minutes in the car.

🚶 Walking
Most of the Strip is Walkable

If you're staying anywhere from Tropicana to Bellagio, you can walk it. MGM Grand is 5 min, Aria 7, Caesars and Bellagio ~15.

🅿️ Parking
Aria's Garage is Your Best Bet

Aria's garage is the closest option. MGM Grand also has parking if you want to walk through the casino. Don't attempt Las Vegas Blvd itself.

Beat the crowd two ways: Leave 45 minutes before the main event ends, or do the opposite and stay until the venue empties (it happens faster than you'd expect). The middle ground — everyone leaving at the same time — is the worst option.

Hotels

Where to Stay

The closer to T-Mobile, the more you'll pay fight weekend. Book 8–12 weeks out. Seriously.

Walking Distance to Arena
  • Park MGM — literally attached to the arena complex
  • New York-New York — across the plaza, same area
  • MGM Grand — 5-min walk, huge casino, tons of food options

These three are the play if proximity matters to you.

Best Value Mid-Strip
  • Excalibur — short Uber or walkable, significantly cheaper rooms
  • Luxor — same situation, same price range

If you're watching from sportsbooks anyway, save money here.

Best Splurge
  • Aria — 7-min walk to T-Mobile, one of the nicer properties on the Strip
  • Vdara — quieter than Aria, no casino floor, all-suite
Off-Strip Value
  • Palms — genuinely underrated, good rooms, cheaper rates
  • Rio — you'll Uber everywhere but the savings are real

Rates spike hard fight weekend. The further out you book, the better.

Tickets

Buying Smart

When & Where to Buy

Buy early when tickets go on sale — prices only go up as fight week approaches. For each event, our weekly guide will have current pricing and availability.

Resale: StubHub and Vivid Seats are the standard options. Check our event guide closer to fight week for what prices are actually doing.

Seat Strategy

Sections 5/6 or 15/16 (5 rows up from floor)

At cage height. The action is in front of you, not below you. Best value with a great view.

Best Value
Floor Seats

Avoid unless you don't mind looking up. The cage is elevated — floor looks like a bad angle when you're actually there.

Skip
Upper Bowl (200s)

Still a great atmosphere and a real view of the octagon. The crowd noise is the same everywhere. Don't let anyone talk you out of the upper deck.

Legit
🏊

Can't get tickets? Stadium Swim at Circa. Already mentioned it. Worth mentioning twice.
